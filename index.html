Frequently asked questions

Question: Is TicketCompass free to use?

Answer: Yes — completely free. We don't charge any fees to compare prices. When you click on a ticket, you'll be redirected to that platform's website to complete your purchase. You'll pay whatever the platform charges (all prices shown already include their fees).

Question: What does "all-in pricing" mean?

Answer: All-in pricing means the price you see already includes every service fee, facility charge, and order fee — no surprises at checkout. We add up the base ticket price plus all fees so you can compare the true cost across platforms.

Question: Which platforms do you compare?

Answer: We currently compare prices from Ticketmaster, StubHub, and SeatGeek — the three largest ticket marketplaces in North America. More platforms may be added in the future.

Question: How often are prices updated?

Answer: Ticket prices are updated regularly to reflect current market listings. Because resale prices fluctuate in real time, the exact price at checkout may vary slightly from what's shown.

Question: Do you sell tickets directly?

Answer: No. TicketCompass is a price comparison tool only. We show you where to find the best deal, then send you directly to the platform to complete your purchase securely.

Question: What sports are covered?

Answer: We currently cover NFL, MLB, NBA, NHL, and FIFA World Cup 2026. We're actively working on adding more sports and events.

TicketCompass is an independent price comparison service. Prices are illustrative and updated regularly but may vary at time of purchase.
